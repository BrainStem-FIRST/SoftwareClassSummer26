Class Requirements

Add onto your Lift class from Day 2

New Feature #1: Battery Independence
- As the battery drains over a match, its voltage drops, which means the same motor power
  produces less actual voltage/force than it did on a fresh battery
- Instead of directly setting motor power from your feedforward + PID output, calculate the
  TOTAL VOLTAGE you want to apply (kG + friction feedforward + PID output), then divide by
  the current battery voltage to get the motor power
- power = totalVoltage / batteryVoltage
- This keeps your lift's behavior consistent no matter how drained the battery is

Add a setBatteryVoltage function
  - takes in 1 argument (double batteryVoltage)
  - sets battery voltage instance data to argument

New Feature #2: Dampening Band
- Right near the target position, small amounts of PID/feedforward output can cause the lift
  to jitter back and forth instead of settling
- A dampening band scales down your total output when the lift is very close to its target,
  so it settles smoothly instead of oscillating

Add a dampeningBand variable on FTC dashboard representing how close (in encoder ticks) the
lift needs to be to its target before dampening kicks in
In your update function
  - calculate the position error
  - calculate a scale factor: the smaller of 1 and (absolute value of error) / dampeningBand
    - this way, once you're within dampeningBand ticks of the target, the scale factor shrinks
      toward 0 as you get closer; otherwise it's just 1 (no scaling)
  - multiply your friction feedforward AND your PID output by this scale factor
  - IMPORTANT: the dampening band is NOT applied to everything. Do NOT scale kG - gravity is
    still pulling on the lift the same amount no matter how close you are to the target, so
    kG is purposefully left out of the scaling
