Add onto your Arm class from Day 2

New Feature: Battery Independence
- As the battery drains over a match, its voltage drops, which means the same motor power
  produces less actual voltage/force than it did on a fresh battery
- Instead of directly setting motor power from your feedforward + PID output, calculate the
  TOTAL VOLTAGE you want to apply (gravity feedforward + friction feedforward + PID output),
  then divide by the current battery voltage to get the motor power
- power = totalVoltage / batteryVoltage
- This keeps your arm's behavior consistent no matter how drained the battery is

Add a setBatteryVoltage function
  - takes in 1 argument (double batteryVoltage)
  - sets battery voltage instance data to argument

New Feature: Deadband
- Right near the target angle, small amounts of friction feedforward/PID output can cause the
  arm to jitter back and forth instead of settling
- A deadband zeroes out that output once the arm is very close to its target, so it settles
  smoothly instead of oscillating

Add a deadbandRadians variable on FTC dashboard representing how close (in radians) the arm
needs to be to its target angle before the deadband kicks in
In your update function
  - calculate the angle error
  - if the absolute value of the angle error is less than deadbandRadians, zero out your
    friction feedforward AND your PID output
  - IMPORTANT: the deadband is NOT applied to everything. Do NOT zero out gravity feedforward -
    the arm still needs to fight gravity even while sitting still at its target angle. Only
    friction feedforward and PID output (which should go to 0 once you're settled anyway) get
    zeroed out
